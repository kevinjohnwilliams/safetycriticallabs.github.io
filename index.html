Why this matters

Three groups need
this certificate.

An SCL certificate answers the question every buyer, operator, and regulator is now asking about AI in critical systems.

AI Developers
Prove your system has been independently assessed
Self-attestation is not enough when lives depend on correct outputs. An SCL certificate demonstrates that a qualified third party reviewed your system against a published standard with verifiable pass/fail criteria. That is a different claim than anything you can make about yourself.
System Integrators and Buyers
Know what you are actually purchasing
When you procure an AI system for safety-critical operations, you assume the risk. An SCL certificate tells you exactly what was assessed, against which standard, at which classification level, and when it expires. You make an informed decision. Uncertified AI leaves you with none of that.
Program Managers and Regulators
Satisfy the AI assurance question before it becomes a problem
Regulatory frameworks for AI in critical systems are forming now. Organizations that can produce a formal certification determination from an independent authority are ahead of every organization that cannot. SCL certification is the answer to the AI assurance question.

Process

Four phases.
One determination.

A structured process that produces a formal certification determination, not a scorecard or a recommendation.

01
Applicability determination
We classify your AI system and establish exactly which requirements apply. You know the scope before any work begins.
02
Evidence package review
You submit documentation, test reports, and verification records. We review each one against the applicable requirements.
03
Technical assessment
A structured audit of your system. Every finding is documented with the specific requirement reference and the success criteria it was measured against.
04
Certification issuance
A formal determination document and, if your system meets the standard, a numbered SCL Certificate of Conformance.
How we work

The standard behind
the certificate.

A certificate is only worth something if the standard behind it is rigorous and the authority issuing it is credible.

◆
Open standard The SCL AI Requirements Framework is publicly available on GitHub and formally published at DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19024421. We certify against a standard anyone can read, audit, and challenge.
◆
Determinations, not recommendations An SCL certificate states what was assessed, against which version of the framework, and at which classification level. It does not claim a system is safe. It claims the system was rigorously evaluated against a defined standard with verifiable pass/fail criteria.
◆
Built from inside operational environments The framework was developed from direct experience building AI systems for safety-critical operations. Requirements address actual failure modes of actual systems, not theoretical concerns from a standards committee working in the abstract.
◆
Certification does not expire quietly AI systems drift. Every SCL certificate includes a defined surveillance schedule. A certificate that goes unsurveilled is flagged, not silently renewed. The standard demands it.
