What we do

A certification authority.
Not a consulting firm.

Safety Critical Labs is a certification authority. The product is the mark. The mark is only valuable if the standard behind it is rigorous and the authority granting it is trusted.

The closest analogies are UL for product safety, DNV for maritime and energy assurance, and ISO registrar bodies for quality management. We do not recommend or advise. We certify against a defined standard that anyone can read, audit, and challenge.

A Safety Critical Labs certificate states what was assessed, against which version of the framework, at which classification level, and what was found. It does not claim a system is safe. It claims the system was rigorously evaluated exactly the way a UL listing works.

What certification gives you

Your AI system is a
black box. We open it.

If you are uncertain about what your AI system is actually doing, or whether it is safe to operate in a critical environment, that uncertainty has a cost. Certification replaces it with a documented, defensible answer.

Black box visibility
Know what your AI is actually doing
Our assessment methodology systematically examines model behavior, output confidence, and decision traceability giving you visibility into the system that documentation alone cannot provide.
Quantified risk
A static risk score, not a gut check
Using a structured risk consequence scoring methodology, we calculate a quantified risk level for your AI system. Not a subjective opinion — a reproducible, defensible number derived from a rigorous scoring framework used in safety-critical programs.
Regulatory confidence
Something to show when someone asks
Whether it's a regulator, a procurement officer, or your own leadership — an SCL certificate is a formal record that your system was rigorously assessed against a published standard. Not a vendor claim. A determination.
Drift detection
Assurance that holds after deployment
AI systems change behavior over time. Certification does not stop at launch. Continuous validation requirements ensure your system is re-assessed when it matters, not just when it is convenient.
Failure mode coverage
Every AI-specific risk addressed
Hallucination, out-of-distribution inputs, adversarial vulnerability, model drift, bias nine structured requirement areas cover the failure modes that make AI in critical systems genuinely dangerous.
Human override assurance
You stay in control
Our human-AI teaming requirements define explicit authority limits and verified override capability so AI augments your team's decisions without silently replacing them.
