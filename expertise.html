The assessment
team behind
every determination.

Every SCL assessment runs with a qualified team: a Lead Assessor who owns the determination, an independent Review Assessor, and domain specialist support where the operational environment demands it. The standard does not change by domain. The expertise brought to assess it does.

Assessment team

Every assessment.
Three roles. No exceptions.

The methodology requires independent review at every phase. A single assessor cannot conduct and verify their own findings. That independence is structural, not optional.

Role 01
Lead Assessor
Runs the assessment end to end. Owns the determination recommendation. Signs the Technical Assessment Report. Makes the final finding on every requirement. Independence from the client organization is mandatory.
Qualification: Domain expertise in AI and ML systems plus safety-critical systems engineering. For the current assessment period, K. Williams serves as Lead Assessor on all engagements.
Role 02
Review Assessor
Conducts an independent evidence review in Phase 2 before the Lead Assessor's findings are shared. Where assessors disagree, the Lead Assessor's determination governs with the dissent on record. No phase skips independent review.
Qualification: Technical background in AI and ML systems. Does not share preliminary findings with Lead Assessor until both independent reviews are complete.
Role 03
Domain Specialist
Brought in for Phase 3 where the operational domain requires regulatory or technical depth beyond the core framework. Aviation, medical devices, automotive, and defense each carry domain-specific regulatory context that the assessment team reflects.
Qualification: Domain-specific certification experience in the relevant regulated industry. Engaged per assessment based on the system's operational environment.
Lead Assessor

What qualifies
the determination.

The SCL framework was not adapted from a governance template. It was built by someone who spent years inside a government human spaceflight program writing the first AI certification requirements for human-rated spacecraft — requirements that are now part of the governing software standard for crewed space programs.

That work produced a government-issued award for exceptional contribution to human spaceflight — recognition that the requirements were consequential enough to matter. The Lead Assessor did not study AI certification from the outside. The requirements were written from inside the operational environment, applied to real vehicles, and reviewed by the program office.

Before that, the same practitioner led a materials engineering lab in automotive manufacturing, deploying deep learning for automated quality decisions in electric motor production at scale. That experience grounds the assessment methodology in what it means to put AI into a real production system with real safety consequences — not a research environment.

Graduate research at a leading engineering institution contributes active academic grounding in AI safety and certification methodology alongside the practical industry background.

The institution

Authority transfers
from person to institution.

SCL is at an early stage where the determination record rests on the Lead Assessor's direct credentials. That is enough to support the first customers and establish the first certification records.

The long-term goal is to transfer that credibility from the individual to the institution: through a formal advisory structure, regulatory engagement, published certification records, and a path toward accreditation under ISO/IEC 17065 for certification bodies.

◆ Practitioner-built standard. Requirements developed from inside the operational environment, not adapted from governance templates by people who have never worked in safety-critical systems.
◆ Open and auditable. The framework is publicly available. Anyone can read the standard before engaging SCL. The assessor who wrote it is the assessor conducting the certification.
◆ Regulatory presence. Active engagement with AI policy processes as they develop across FAA, FDA, and defense acquisition. First-mover certification records are how standards get adopted.
◆ Accreditation path. ISO/IEC 17065 gap assessment is on the roadmap. The goal is a regulatory instrument, not just a credible third party.
